Framework 1: The "Stay or Go" Decision Matrix

The most paralyzing question in career transitions is simple: "Should I stay or should I go?"

Most people get stuck here for months—or years—because they're trying to find a "perfect" answer that doesn't exist. The truth is, there's no perfect answer. There's only the right answer for you, right now.

This matrix helps you cut through the noise and make a clear decision.

Step 1: Rate Your Current Situation

On a scale of 1-10, rate each of these areas:

Energy

Does your work give you energy or drain you?

Score: ___/10

Growth

Are you learning and developing?

Score: ___/10

Impact

Does your work feel meaningful?

Score: ___/10

Alignment

Does it fit your values and life goals?

Score: ___/10

Step 2: Calculate Your Score

  • 32-40: Your role is working. Focus on optimization, not exit.
  • 24-31: There's friction. Identify the 1-2 areas dragging you down.
  • 16-23: Significant misalignment. It's time to explore options seriously.
  • Below 16: This isn't sustainable. Start planning your exit.

🎯 Action Step

Complete the matrix above right now. Don't overthink it—go with your gut. Your first instinct is usually right.

Framework 2: 5 Signs You're Ready for a Career Change

Sometimes the signs are obvious. Other times, they sneak up on us. Here are the 5 indicators that you're genuinely ready (not just having a bad week):

Sign 1: Sunday Dread Has Become Permanent

It's not occasional stress—it's a persistent heaviness that doesn't lift even on vacation. You've tried fixing it (new project, different team, working less) and nothing works.

Sign 2: You've Outgrown the Role

You've mastered what you're doing. There's no challenge left, no growth on the horizon, and the ceiling is visible. You're operating at maybe 60% of your potential.

Sign 3: Your Values Have Shifted

What mattered to you 5 years ago (title, money, prestige) doesn't matter as much now. You're craving something different—maybe impact, autonomy, or work-life integration.

Sign 4: You Keep Having the Same Conversation

With your partner, friends, therapist, or even just yourself. "I should really think about leaving." If you've been having this conversation for 6+ months, that's data.

Sign 5: Your Body Is Sending Signals

Chronic fatigue, insomnia, anxiety, or health issues that mysteriously improve when you're away from work. Your body often knows before your mind does.

Key Insight

You don't need to check all 5 boxes. If you recognize yourself in 2-3 of these, it's worth taking your career questions seriously.

Framework 3: The Career Clarity Framework

Once you know you're ready for a change, the next question is: "Change to what?"

This is where most people get stuck. They think they need to figure out their "passion" or find the "perfect" next role. Wrong approach.

Instead, use this framework to identify what actually matters:

The 4 Pillars of Career Clarity

1. Strengths

What do you do effortlessly that others find hard? What do people consistently come to you for?

2. Values

What's non-negotiable for you? Autonomy? Impact? Stability? Work-life balance? Be honest.

3. Interests

What topics can you talk about for hours? What do you read/watch/listen to in your free time?

4. Environment

What conditions help you thrive? Remote/office? Team/solo? Structured/flexible? Fast/steady?

The Sweet Spot

Your ideal next move sits at the intersection of all four. It leverages your strengths, aligns with your values, engages your interests, and fits your preferred environment.

🎯 Action Step

Spend 15 minutes writing down 3 answers for each pillar. Don't filter—just brainstorm. You'll refine later.

Framework 4: How to Pivot Without Starting Over

Here's what most people get wrong about career pivots: they think they need to start from scratch.

The truth? You've already built a foundation of skills, relationships, and credibility. The goal isn't to throw that away—it's to redirect it.

The Pivot Ladder

Think of career change as a ladder with 5 rungs. You don't have to jump to the bottom—you can step down gradually:

  1. Same Role, Different Company — Least change, but solves cultural/leadership issues
  2. Same Industry, Different Role — Leverage domain expertise in a new function
  3. Same Role, Different Industry — Apply your skills in a new context
  4. Adjacent Move — Combine elements of your experience in a new way
  5. Complete Reinvention — Start fresh (rarely necessary)

The 70% Rule

The most successful pivots transfer at least 70% of your existing skills and experience. Look for roles where you can apply what you already know while learning the new 30%.

Key Insight

Most people overestimate how much they need to change. Often, a smaller pivot (Rung 1-3) solves the problem better than a complete reinvention.

Framework 5: Your 7-Day Next Steps Action Plan

Clarity without action is just daydreaming. Here's your week-by-week plan to start moving forward:

Day 1-2: Reflect

  • Complete the Stay or Go Decision Matrix
  • Write down your scores for each of the 4 Clarity Pillars
  • Identify which Pivot Ladder rung makes sense for you

Day 3-4: Research

  • List 5 roles/companies that interest you (based on your clarity work)
  • Find 3 people on LinkedIn who have made a similar pivot
  • Read 2-3 articles about transitions in your target direction

Day 5-6: Reach Out

  • Send 2 coffee chat requests to people in roles you're curious about
  • Tell 1 trusted person about your exploration (accountability partner)
  • Block 2 hours in your calendar for "career exploration" next week

Day 7: Decide

  • Review what you've learned this week
  • Decide: Am I ready to explore this further with support?
  • If yes, book a discovery call to discuss next steps
